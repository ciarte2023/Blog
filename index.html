Landslides

About Causes Solutions & preventions How to survive

About

Landslides, sometimes called landslips, are when the ground and rocks move down a hill or slope. It happens naturally and can be big or small, sometimes causing a lot of damage to towns and things like roads.

Causes

Landslides can happen for different reasons, but some of the main ones are:

  • Heavy Rain: When it rains a lot, the ground can get very wet and unstable, which might start a landslide.

  • Steep Hills: If a hill is really steep, things are more likely to slide down because of gravity.

  • Earthquakes and Volcanoes: Sometimes, when the ground shakes because of an earthquake or volcano, it can make landslides happen.

  • People: Things like cutting down trees, building, or digging for stuff can make the ground less stable and more likely to have landslides.

Knowing why landslides happen is important to stop them or make them less dangerous.

Solutions & preventions

To stop landslides, we need to do a few things:

  • Early Warning: We can use special machines to watch the ground and tell us if a landslide might happen soon. Then, we can tell people to be safe.

  • Build Things: Making strong walls, fixing slopes, and building drains can help prevent landslides or make them less bad.

  • Plants Help: Putting plants on hills and slopes can make the ground more stable and less likely to have landslides.

  • Plan Carefully: We should think hard about where to build things and make rules to avoid dangerous places.

  • Tell Everyone: It's important to teach people about landslides so they know what to do to stay safe.

How to survive

  • Move away from the threat - don't approach an active landslide.

  • Escape vertically by moving upstairs or even on countertops to avoidbeing swept away.

  • Don't stay close to mountains.

  • Identify and relocate to interior, ideally unfurnished,areas of buildingthat offer more protection.

  • Open downhill doors and windows to let debris escape.

  • Don't die.
